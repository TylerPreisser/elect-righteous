### KEY CONTROVERSY: SB 360 / Prescription Drug Pricing

**The bill:** Senate Bill 360 -- a bipartisan Senate bill to bring greater state regulation to prescription drug middlemen (PBMs -- Pharmacy Benefit Managers) and improve drug pricing transparency.

**Hawkins' actions as Speaker:**
- Used his power as Speaker to **order SB 360 transferred between the House Insurance and Health committees FOUR TIMES** since February 25, 2026 -- with no committee action taken
- When ~50 frustrated House Republicans backed by dozens of House Democrats prepared to force the bill to the floor without Hawkins' blessing, **Hawkins and Majority Leader Chris Croft suddenly ended the House session** on March 19, 2026
- The bill's contents were eventually inserted into Senate Bill 20 during a conference committee hearing

**The override:** The House ultimately **passed the PBM regulation measure 104-17** -- a veto-proof, overwhelmingly bipartisan vote that demonstrated Hawkins was drastically out of step with his own caucus on this issue.

**Significance:** Hawkins, a 30-year insurance industry executive running for Insurance Commissioner, actively blocked legislation to lower prescription drug costs -- the exact kind of consumer protection the Insurance Commissioner is supposed to champion.
