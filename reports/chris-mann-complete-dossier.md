## 3. LAW ENFORCEMENT CAREER & THE ACCIDENT

### Police Career

- Mann followed in his father's footsteps and chose a career in law enforcement.
- In **1998**, Mann became the youngest officer in the Lawrence Police Department while still a junior at the University of Kansas. He was just **21 years old**.
- He served on the Lawrence PD conducting patrol work, including routine traffic stops.

### The Drunk Driver Accident -- January 11, 2002

**Date:** January 11, 2002, approximately 3:30 a.m.

**What happened:** Officer Chris Mann had pulled over a vehicle for a broken taillight. While standing outside his parked cruiser, an intoxicated driver slammed into his parked police cruiser at approximately **50 mph**. The force of the collision threw Mann into the back of the SUV he had pulled over and deposited him **30 feet away** on the side of the road.

**Injuries:** The crash caused severe injuries (specific injuries not publicly detailed beyond "severe") that ended his career in uniform. He was approximately 24-25 years old at the time.

**Recovery:** Mann endured **years of intense physical therapy** and battled the emotional toll of his injuries. The recovery period was prolonged and grueling.

**Career Impact:** Mann was forced to retire from the Lawrence Police Department at approximately age 25. The crash "changed the trajectory of Chris's life."

**What came after:** Rather than giving up on public service, Mann realized he could continue to fight crime as an attorney in the courtroom. He enrolled in Washburn University School of Law.

## 8. 2022 RACE: FULL ANALYSIS

### Official Results (Final Certified)

| Candidate | Party | Votes | Percentage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Kris Kobach** | Republican | **506,817** | **50.80%** |
| **Chris Mann** | Democrat | **490,925** | **49.20%** |

**Margin:** Kobach won by **15,892 votes** (some reports say 17,363 due to rounding/provisional counting stages) -- just **1.6 percentage points**.

This was the **closest statewide race in Kansas** in the 2022 election cycle.

### 2022 Democratic Primary

Mann ran unopposed in the Democratic primary, receiving **248,846 votes (100%)**.

### County-Level Results

**Counties Won by Mann (5 of 105):**

| County | Significance |
|---|---|
| **Johnson County** | Most populous county in Kansas (suburban Kansas City). Mann's strongest county. |
| **Douglas County** | Home of Lawrence (Mann's residence) and the University of Kansas |
| **Shawnee County** | Home of Topeka, the state capital |
| **Riley County** | Home of Manhattan and Kansas State University |
| **Lyon County** | Home of Emporia and Emporia State University |

**Key County Details:**
- Mann had a combined **~90,000-vote advantage** in Johnson, Douglas, and Shawnee counties -- but it was not enough to overcome the rural GOP vote across the remaining 100 counties.
- **Sedgwick County** (Wichita, second-most populous): Kobach won by **fewer than 1,800 votes** out of nearly **149,000 cast**. This was extremely close and a major missed opportunity for Mann.

### Ellis County (Hays) Results

**Confidence Level: MEDIUM** -- Ellis County was not among Mann's five winning counties, meaning Kobach carried it. Ellis County is historically Republican. The Kansas Secretary of State's official 2022 General Election vote totals PDF (https://sos.ks.gov/elections/22elec/2022-General-Official-Vote-Totals.pdf) contains the county-by-county breakdown, but specific Ellis County numbers were not extracted in available search results.

**Assessment:** Given that Ellis County voted Republican in every statewide race in 2022, Kobach almost certainly carried Ellis County by a significant margin (likely 60-40 or wider). Mann's strength was entirely in the urban/suburban I-35 corridor (Johnson, Douglas, Shawnee) plus the two university counties (Riley, Lyon).

### Why Mann Lost in 2022

1. **Rural Kansas is deep red.** Winning 5 of 105 counties is actually strong for a Democrat in a non-presidential year in Kansas.
2. **Sedgwick County (Wichita) was the missed opportunity.** Kobach won it by only 1,800 votes. If Mann had flipped Sedgwick, he still would have needed more, but it would have narrowed the gap significantly.
3. **Name recognition deficit.** Kobach is one of the most well-known political figures in Kansas. Mann was a first-time candidate with no prior political profile.
4. **Straight-ticket Republican voting in rural areas** overwhelmed Mann's urban margins.

## 16. WHY HE THINKS HE CAN WIN THIS TIME

Based on all available evidence, Mann's theory of the case for 2026 rests on several pillars:

### 1. The 2022 Margin Was Razor-Thin
- Lost by only 1.6 points (15,892 votes) in a cycle where he was an unknown first-time candidate.
- It was the closest statewide race in Kansas in 2022.
- His logic: if he can find ~16,000 more votes across the state, he wins.

### 2. Name Recognition Is No Longer a Problem
- In 2022, Mann was a complete unknown running against one of the most recognized political names in Kansas.
- In 2026, he has statewide name ID from the 2022 race. Voters know who he is.

### 3. Fundraising Momentum
- More than doubled his fundraising at this point compared to 2022 ($537K vs. $205K).
- 89% of contributions from Kansans (grassroots signal).
- 2,500 individual contributors.

### 4. Kobach's Record as AG Is Now Attackable
- In 2022, Kobach was running on promises. In 2026, Mann can attack Kobach's actual record in office.
- Kobach has been criticized for using the AG's office for political crusades (immigration lawsuits, transgender sports bans, fighting federal agencies) rather than bread-and-butter law enforcement.
- Mann frames this as "politician vs. law enforcement officer."

### 5. Kansas Political Environment
- The 2022 Value Them Both referendum showed Kansas voters are more moderate than the state's Republican leadership on key issues.
- If 2026 features a competitive governor's race or other high-profile down-ballot dynamics, Democratic turnout could be higher.

### 6. Sedgwick County (Wichita) Is the Swing
- Kobach won Sedgwick by fewer than 1,800 votes in 2022. This is Mann's most obvious target for vote gains.

## 17. HIS PITCH TO MODERATE REPUBLICANS

Mann's crossover appeal strategy is built on several deliberate elements:

### The "Law Enforcement, Not Politics" Frame
- Mann leads with his identity as a **police officer and prosecutor** -- credentials that traditionally resonate with Republican voters.
- He frames Kobach as a "politician" using the AG office for personal ambition, contrasting himself as a public safety professional.

### The Bipartisan Attorney Coalition
- In 2022, **"Kansas Attorneys for Mann"** included **140+ Republican, Democratic, and Independent attorneys** who publicly endorsed Mann.
- Their message was explicitly aimed at Republicans: "Setting aside his policies and positions, **Kris Kobach is not a good attorney**."
- They cited Kobach's $1.9 million in fees and expenses his office was forced to pay after his 2018 voting rights trial, and the federal judge's order requiring Kobach to take **six hours of remedial law classes**.

### Gun-Owning, Pro-Second-Amendment Democrat
- Mann emphasizes he is a responsible gun owner who supports the Second Amendment.
- This positioning is designed to neutralize a traditional Republican attack line.

### Respecting the Will of Voters
- On abortion, Mann doesn't lead with ideology -- he leads with the referendum result. "The people of Kansas have clearly spoken." This gives moderate Republicans permission to support him without feeling they're endorsing a progressive agenda.

### Pro-Law-Enforcement Credentials
- A former police officer who was nearly killed in the line of duty has instant credibility with law-and-order voters that most Democrats lack.
- His MADD chairmanship reinforces this tough-on-crime image.

### Avoiding National Democratic Branding
- Mann keeps his campaign focused on Kansas issues and avoids national partisan framing.
- He does not lead with hot-button national Democratic positions.

## 18. STRENGTHS & VULNERABILITIES ASSESSMENT

### Strengths

1. **Compelling personal story** -- The drunk driver accident, recovery, and pivot to law is a powerful narrative that humanizes him and demonstrates resilience.
2. **Law enforcement background** -- Rare for a Democrat. Gives him credibility on public safety that most Democratic candidates lack.
3. **MADD national chairman** -- High-profile leadership role in a universally respected, non-partisan organization.
4. **Nearly won in 2022** -- Proven he can run close in a red state. Not a theoretical candidate.
5. **Outraises Kobach** -- Demonstrates strong donor support and grassroots energy.
6. **Kobach is a polarizing incumbent** -- Kobach energizes the Democratic base and alienates some moderate Republicans.
7. **Bipartisan appeal** -- 140+ attorneys across party lines endorsed him in 2022.
8. **Pro-gun, pro-law-enforcement Democrat** -- Neutralizes standard GOP attack vectors.

### Vulnerabilities

1. **He's still a Democrat in Kansas.** Kansas has not elected a Democratic Attorney General since Robert Stephan switched parties in the 1970s. The structural disadvantage is enormous.
2. **Never held elected office.** While he ran in 2022, he has never actually governed or legislated.
3. **Lawrence residency.** Lawrence is perceived as the most liberal city in Kansas. This could cut against his moderate appeal in rural areas.
4. **Limited social media presence.** 666 Instagram followers and 2,476 Facebook likes suggest a weak digital operation.
5. **Won only 5 of 105 counties in 2022.** His path to victory depends entirely on running up urban/suburban margins while Kobach's support is geographically broad.
6. **Sedgwick County is not guaranteed.** He lost it by 1,800 votes in 2022 but still needs to flip it AND gain elsewhere.
7. **His law firm focuses on DUI defense.** While his MADD work emphasizes fighting drunk driving, his private practice *defending* DUI clients could be used as an attack line by opponents (though this is standard for criminal defense attorneys).
8. **No clear church identity.** In Kansas, where faith matters to many voters, the absence of a visible church affiliation may leave him unable to connect with religiously motivated voters on a personal level.
