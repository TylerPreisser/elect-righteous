### COVID-19 Mask Mandate (2020) -- CONSISTENT DISSENTER

Mason Ruder was the most consistent voice against Hays's mask mandate. His voting record:

| Date | Vote | Result | Notes |
|------|------|--------|-------|
| **July 2020** | **AGAINST** initial mandatory face mask ordinance | Passed 3-1 or 3-2 | Said he will wear a mask as recommended by his doctor but "believes the decision should not be a mandate" |
| **Aug 27, 2020** | **AGAINST** extension | Passed 3-2 | Ruder and Michael Berges voted against |
| **Oct 2, 2020** | **AGAINST** extension | Passed 3-1 | Sole dissenter on this vote |
| **Subsequent votes** | **AGAINST** further extensions | Passed with Ruder dissenting | Consistently voted against through the ordinance's duration |

**Significance**: Ruder's mask mandate dissent is a defining feature of his political identity. He framed it as a personal liberty issue -- he would voluntarily wear a mask but opposed government compulsion. This is strongly aligned with conservative/libertarian values and earned him significant support from the liberty-minded electorate.

### R9 Ranch Water Transfer Project -- CHAMPION

This is the signature infrastructure issue of Ruder's tenure:

- **Background**: Hays bought the 6,700-acre R9 Ranch in Edwards County in 1995, intending to use its 30 water rights for public water supply for Hays and Russell
- In 2015-2016, the cities applied to switch water use from irrigation to municipal and transfer 2,000 acre-feet of water
- Edwards County passed zoning restrictions attempting to block the transfer
- **Ruder's testimony**: Testified before the House Local Government Committee on January 28, 2026 in Topeka
  - Argued Edwards County's zoning restrictions "duplicate existing state water law"
  - Stated: "The intent was clear... the goal was to force Hays and Russell to navigate a second regulatory process controlled entirely by the same county that is attempting to stop the project"
- **Result**: Rep. Barb Wasinger sponsored HB 2433, which Governor Kelly signed March 20, 2026 -- a major victory for Hays. The bill places water transfer authority exclusively with the State's Chief Engineer and Water Transfer Hearing Panel, nullifying Edwards County's local restrictions
