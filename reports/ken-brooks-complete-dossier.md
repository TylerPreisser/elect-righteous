## KEY VOTES & POSITIONS

### HMS Mascot "Indians" Vote -- VOTED AGAINST (April 2024)

The board voted 5-2 to change the Hays Middle School mascot to "Indians." Ken Brooks and Meagan Zampieri-Lillpopp were the two dissenters.

- Brooks recommended the topic wait to be voted on until the next meeting so that community members would have more time to bring forward concerns and board members would have more time to think about the issue
- He did not argue the mascot was inappropriate -- he argued for more deliberation and community input before a vote
- Hays High School retained the "Indians" mascot for the high school after a separate process
- Sources: [HaysPost: Board votes on mascot](https://hayspost.com/posts/5b2cf14f-06bc-4086-9693-c33581fe6a7c) | [Tiger Media Network](http://tigermedianet.com/?p=78688)

### Gender-Neutral Bathrooms at New Hays High School -- SUPPORTED ORIGINAL DESIGN

- The new $105-115M Hays High School included bathroom designs where about 30% of bathrooms would be private stalls with floor-to-ceiling doors and shared sink areas for both female and male students
- Board members Allen Park and Curt Vajnar raised objections and pushed for the topic to be revisited
- Board President Ken Brooks stated the board had previously approved the bathroom plans and pushed back on reopening the discussion
- Brooks stated: "The whole bond process was done out in public" with "community involvement was key"
- When Allen Park tried to make a motion to amend the agenda to add the bathroom discussion again, Brooks (as president) ruled he could not because the same motion had failed previously
- Sources: [Tiger Media Network](https://tigermedianet.com/?p=78383) | [HaysPost](https://hayspost.com/posts/677ac412-2adb-48bd-85f8-bbd5f05bb92f)

### Nepotism Policy -- SUPPORTED SUPERINTENDENT

- Board members Allen Park and Curt Vajnar raised objections about the district hiring Superintendent Ron Wilson's wife as a teacher
- Board VP Ken Brooks said: "This is the same discussion we had last month that got out of hand"
- Brooks agreed with the superintendent, saying it was more an issue with the policy rather than the personnel transactions
- The existing nepotism policy allowed family members of administrators to be hired as long as: (1) the board is notified of the relationship, and (2) the family member is not supervised/evaluated by that administrator
- Source: [HaysPost: Nepotism debate](https://hayspost.com/posts/9fb91da0-f9e5-4d80-a065-c5c2727c6fb8)

### $148 Million Bond Issue -- STRONG SUPPORTER

- The bond passed in May 2022, funding:
  - New $105M+ Hays High School (226,000 sq ft) -- groundbreaking completed, ribbon cut
  - O'Loughlin Elementary renovation ($14.49M, Nabholz Construction, came in $172K under budget)
  - Roosevelt Elementary and other projects
- Brooks has consistently championed the bond projects
- After 2025 re-election: "I hope to keep us on track as we finish the bond project"
- As president, noted building costs were "probably about 40 percent more than in 2021 or 2022"
- Total project cost for new HHS reached $114,963,731 after change orders
- Sources: [HaysPost: Ground broken on new HHS](https://hayspost.com/posts/1dbbae4c-f879-499e-bea0-88777d5eeb41) | [HaysPost: Bond budget scrutiny](https://hayspost.com/posts/87f9b442-e928-4c9d-bb85-03acad9d2a3f)

### Naming Rights -- ADVOCATED FOR ACCEPTING GOLDEN BELT BANK DONATION

- Golden Belt Bank offered $200,000 for naming rights to the new Hays High football field (10-year term)
- Bank set a September 15 deadline
- Brooks made a motion to add the naming discussion to the agenda -- failed 3-3 (Brooks, Ruth Ruder, and Zampieri-Lillpopp voted in favor)
- Brooks said he did not think Golden Belt Bank had "any ill intentions" and they were "trying to help our community and help our school district"
- Board President Curt Vajnar removed the discussion from the agenda, and the board eventually let the offer expire
- Source: [HaysPost: Naming rights paused](https://hayspost.com/posts/a90fb672-f7e5-4826-b5f3-1decd214e83c)

### HHS/Roosevelt Construction Contract -- INVOLVED IN HEATED DISCUSSION

- The school board approved the HHS and Roosevelt construction contract in what was described as a "heated" discussion
- Source: [HaysPost: HHS, Roosevelt contract](https://hayspost.com/posts/6942435f-2284-422c-9ed9-5bf7d68c4984)

## ASSESSMENT

### Strengths
1. **Deep faith commitment**: 14+ years leading children's ministry at Celebration Community Church -- this is not casual involvement, it is a defining part of his life
2. **Community service**: 18 years coordinating Thanksgiving food drives -- genuine servant leadership
3. **Single parent perspective**: Raised three daughters through Hays schools as a single parent; brings real understanding of family challenges
4. **Fiscal responsibility**: Consistently supports responsible budget management while completing bond projects
5. **Stability**: Long-tenure board member, Insurance Planning employee (18+ years), church ministry leader (14+ years) -- he is a steady, committed person
6. **Kiwanis membership**: Additional community service orientation

### Concerns / Areas of Note
1. **Gender-neutral bathroom stance**: Supported the original bathroom design with shared-sink private stalls at new HHS, which some community members found concerning. Used procedural authority to block further discussion.
2. **Nepotism policy**: Sided with the superintendent on hiring the superintendent's wife as a teacher, rather than with board members who raised conflict-of-interest concerns
3. **Naming rights**: Tried to accept the $200,000 Golden Belt Bank donation for naming the football field, which some saw as commercializing public education facilities
4. **No public social media**: Makes it harder to verify his personal positions on issues beyond school board matters

### Faith Assessment

- Coordinating Thanksgiving food drives through the ministerial alliance connects him to the broader faith community
- His church (Celebration Community Church / celebratejesus.org) is an evangelical/nondenominational congregation
