## 10. THE 2018 PRIMARY -- 345 VOTES {#10-the-2018-primary}

### The Race
- August 7, 2018: Republican primary between incumbent Governor Colyer and Secretary of State Kris Kobach
- **Over 313,000 votes cast**
- Initial margin: Kobach led by 191 votes on election night
- After additional absentee and provisional ballots: Kobach's lead expanded to approximately **345 votes**
- **The closest statewide GOP primary in Kansas history**
- **The narrowest defeat of an incumbent governor in a primary in U.S. history**

### The Trump Factor
- Donald Trump had endorsed Kris Kobach before the primary
- This endorsement was widely seen as the decisive factor in the razor-thin result

### Colyer's Response
- August 14, 2018: Colyer **conceded** without requesting a recount or filing legal challenges
- Called Kobach to congratulate him
- Committed to keeping the seat in Republican hands
- This gracious concession is often cited as evidence of his character and party loyalty

### Aftermath
- Kobach went on to **lose the general election** to Democrat Laura Kelly
- Kansas had an opportunity to retain a Republican governor through Colyer, but the primary loss and Kobach's general election defeat handed the governorship to Democrats
- This outcome fuels Colyer's 2026 argument that he is the electability candidate

## 26. STRENGTHS & VULNERABILITIES ASSESSMENT {#26-assessment}

### STRENGTHS

1. **Unmatched humanitarian credentials.** No other candidate in this race -- or virtually any American political race -- can claim to have been the only surgeon in southern Rwanda during a genocide. His 35+ years of war zone medical service across 20+ countries is extraordinary and genuine.

2. **Executive experience.** He is the only candidate who has actually served as Governor of Kansas. He managed a $17 billion budget and 40,000 employees.

3. **Financial turnaround narrative.** Turning a $500 million deficit into a $900 million surplus without raising taxes is a powerful talking point with fiscal conservatives.

4. **Hays roots.** For western Kansas voters specifically, Colyer is one of their own -- born and raised in Hays, TMP graduate, father was a beloved Hays dentist for 30 years, family raised horses on a small farm. He is the only Kansas governor from Hays in state history.

5. **Medical authority.** As a board-certified surgeon, he brings credibility on healthcare policy that no other candidate can match.

6. **Name recognition.** Leads all candidates at 35.1% in Kansas Speaks polling.

7. **Trump alignment.** Serving as Trump's 2024 Kansas Campaign Chair gives him Trump-world credibility in a primary where all candidates are competing for the Trump mantle.

8. **Cancer survivor narrative.** Overcoming prostate cancer adds a personal resilience story.

9. **Family story.** Wife Ruth's impressive scientific career (NIH Genetic Therapy Team), their joint service in Rwanda, and three daughters present a strong family image.

10. **Catholic faith.** Demonstrated through actions (TMP education, parish membership, humanitarian service, protecting Catholic Charities) rather than just words.

### VULNERABILITIES

1. **Brownback association.** Seven years as Brownback's Lieutenant Governor during the disastrous tax experiment is the single biggest political liability. Opponents will continue to tie him to that record.

2. **Third attempt.** This is his third try for the governor's mansion (2018 loss, 2022 withdrawal, 2026). Some voters may see this as inability to close.

3. **2018 Trump endorsement went to Kobach.** In the 2018 primary, Trump endorsed Kobach over Colyer. While Colyer has since aligned with Trump, opponents could use this history.

4. **Overland Park residence.** He grew up in Hays but lives in the Kansas City suburbs. Western Kansas voters may see him as having left.

5. **Self-funding criticism.** The $1.05 million in personal loans, while showing commitment, also invites "millionaire candidate" attacks.

6. **Abortion position vs. Kansas voters.** Kansas voters rejected an abortion amendment in 2022 by a significant margin. Colyer's strong pro-life stance may be out of step with the general electorate (though potentially advantageous in the GOP primary).

7. **"Wokeism" definition weakness.** When pressed to define "wokeism" at FHSU, his answer was vague and criticized by media as lacking substance.

8. **Health questions.** While he says he's recovered from prostate cancer, opponents or voters may quietly wonder about long-term health.
