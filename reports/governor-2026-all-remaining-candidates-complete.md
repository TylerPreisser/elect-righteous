### THE KELLY PRESSURE CAMPAIGN (Full Story)

In August 2025, Holscher publicly accused Gov. Laura Kelly's staff of running a pressure campaign to clear the Democratic primary field for a preferred candidate -- originally Lawrence attorney Chris Mann (who narrowly lost the 2022 AG race to Kris Kobach).

**What happened:** Holscher said she was among three Democrats in the Kansas Senate told by the Kelly administration to "stand aside" so Mann could prevail in the primary. Holscher characterized it as bullying.

**Kelly's staff response:** Will Lawrence, Kelly's chief of staff, framed it differently: "No one told them, 'You can't run.' They can make their own decisions" and "It was never forceful." He said Kelly merely met with potential candidates and "offered advice."

**Outcome:** Mann ultimately chose to run for attorney general again instead of governor. Kelly then endorsed Corson in November 2025. Holscher stayed in the race.

**Significance:** This episode reveals a genuine tension in the Kansas Democratic Party. Holscher feels she was unfairly pressured, and Kelly's eventual endorsement of Corson over Holscher seems to validate her concerns about establishment favoritism.
