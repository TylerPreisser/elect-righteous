## LEGISLATIVE RECORD -- KEY VOTES & BILLS

### Bills Sponsored

**HB 2433 (2026) -- R9 Ranch Water Transfer Bill -- SIGNATURE LEGISLATION**
- **Sponsored by Wasinger**
- Clarifies county authority over water transfers, placing authority with the Chief Engineer and Water Transfer Hearing Panel
- Nullifies Edwards County's zoning restrictions that were blocking Hays's R9 Ranch water project
- **Governor Kelly signed into law March 20, 2026**
- This is a major win for her district -- Hays has been fighting for this water transfer for 11 years
- Source: [KS Legislature HB2433](https://www.kslegislature.gov/li/b2025_26/measures/hb2433/)

### Key Votes on Major Issues

#### Transgender / Bathroom Bill (SB 244) -- VOTED YES + FORCED VOTE
- **February 2026**: Made the "motion of the previous question" -- a procedural move that stops all debate and forces an immediate vote
- This was a tactically aggressive move that prevented further Democratic opposition arguments
- **House voted 87-37 to override Governor Kelly's veto** -- bill became law
- The bill requires people to use bathrooms in public places aligned with sex assigned at birth
- Also requires biological sex on driver's licenses and birth certificates
- Sources: [Kansas Reflector](https://kansasreflector.com/2026/02/18/house-overrides-veto-bathroom-bill-limiting-access-to-kansas-facilities-will-become-law/) | [KCUR](https://www.kcur.org/politics-elections-and-government/2026-02-18/kansas-republicans-transgender-bathroom-bill-veto-override)

#### Abortion Reporting (HB 2749) -- VOTED YES
- April 2024 vote
- Requires reporting of the reasons for each abortion performed at medical care facilities
- Passed the House
- Source: [KS Legislature vote record](https://www.kslegislature.gov/li_2024/b2023_24/measures/vote_view/je_20240429095526_260190/)

#### Food Sales Tax Elimination (HB 2106) -- VOTED YES
- Voted to eliminate food sales tax and increase property tax exemptions for seniors and all Kansans
- This is a bipartisan-popular position -- food sales tax reduction benefits all constituents
- Source: [barbforkansas.com](https://barbforkansas.com/)

## ASSESSMENT

### Strengths
1. **21+ years of continuous elected service**: City Commissioner, Mayor, County Commissioner, State Representative -- the longest and most comprehensive local government resume of any Hays-area official
2. **First woman**: First woman elected Ellis County Commissioner -- a historic achievement
3. **Won against a nine-term incumbent**: Defeated Eber Phelps in 2018 by just 35 votes, then steadily built her margin to 73% -- shows political skill and constituent connection
4. **Deep Catholic faith with education**: Theology degree + active Lector/Eucharistic Minister at St. Joseph. This is informed, practiced faith, not casual affiliation
5. **Business owner**: Ellis County Abstract and Title + W.T.C. Enterprises -- understands small business and property markets
6. **HB 2433 water bill**: Delivered a historic win for Hays on the R9 Ranch water transfer after 11 years of fighting
7. **Committee power**: Chairs General Government Budget and Joint Admin Rules -- significant institutional influence
8. **Pro-life, pro-gun, pro-school choice**: Consistent conservative record
9. **Bathroom bill procedural force**: Showed willingness to use institutional power to advance conservative priorities (motion of the previous question)

### Concerns / Areas of Note
1. **Bathroom bill procedure**: Using the "motion of the previous question" to cut off debate is legitimate but aggressive -- it prevents opponents from making their case publicly. Some constituents may see this as stifling democratic discourse.
2. **Business interests / title company**: As a title company owner and property management firm owner, she has potential conflicts of interest on any legislation affecting real estate, property taxes, or title insurance regulation. Worth monitoring.
3. **Tom Wasinger identification**: Her husband Tom's exact current role is not fully clear from public records. The FHSU supervisor match is probable but not confirmed.
4. **No strong opponent**: Her margins have grown so large (73% in 2024) that she may face insufficient accountability pressure. Comfortable incumbents sometimes become less responsive.

### Faith Assessment
Barbara Wasinger's faith is among the strongest documented in local Hays-area politics:
- **Theology degree** from a Catholic university -- she has formally studied the faith
- **Active liturgical ministry** (Lector + Eucharistic Minister) -- she practices what she studied
- **St. Joseph Catholic Church** membership -- one of the two major Catholic parishes in Hays
- Her policy positions (pro-life, traditional values on gender) are consistent with Catholic teaching
- She represents the educated, engaged Catholic laywoman model
