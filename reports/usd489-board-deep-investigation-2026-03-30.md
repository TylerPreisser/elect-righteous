## Date: March 30, 2026

---

# BOARD OVERVIEW

The USD 489 Board of Education consists of seven at-large members serving four-year terms. The board is officially nonpartisan. Board Clerk: Jess Reling (jreling@usd489.com). The board oversees a district that recently completed a **$143.5 million bond** to build a new Hays High School ($115M, opened August 2025), convert the old high school into a middle school, and renovate elementary schools.

**Key dynamics on this board:** There is a clear factional split. Allen Park and Derek Yarmer (and sometimes Curt Vajnar) form a fiscal oversight/transparency bloc that frequently votes against the administration majority. Ruth Ruder, Ken Brooks, and Jayme Goetz generally vote with the administration. Craig Pallister is the newest member (January 2026) and his alignment is developing.

## Basic Information

- **Occupation:** Business owner (partner, Hays K-Lawn), rancher/farmer (small farm near Antonino), retired teacher
- **Education:** BS and MS from Kansas State University; School Administration certificate from Fort Hays State University

## Career Background
Vajnar taught agriculture education at Hays High School for **31 years**. He was named **Kansas Teacher of the Year** by KNEA -- the youngest teacher to win that honor at the time. He was also the only nationally board-certified agriculture teacher in the state. He had teaching endorsements in Agriculture Education, Biology, Drivers Education, and Administration. He chose never to pursue an administrator role despite holding the certificate. Retired around 2020.

## Board Positions & Voting Record

**Transparency Advocate:** Vajnar has consistently pushed for more transparency. He has advocated that all board meetings, work sessions, and special meetings should be scheduled in evenings so the community can attend, and all should be videoed and made publicly available.

**Bond Oversight:** Vajnar was concerned that a construction contract was signed before the board officially approved it, saying he "did not believe he had an opportunity to educate himself on the contract." He voted against the Hays High/Roosevelt construction contract (5-2 vote, Park and Vajnar against).

**Nepotism Opponent:** Vajnar opposed the hiring of Superintendent Ron Wilson's wife Andrea as a teacher, asking that the personnel transaction be removed from the consent agenda for separate discussion. Voted against 5-2 (Park and Vajnar dissenting).

**Naming Rights:** Vajnar blocked the Golden Belt Bank $200,000 football field naming rights deal, removing it from the agenda. He said the bank was not offering enough for a 10-year contract and believed all businesses should have an equal opportunity to bid.

## CONTROVERSIES

### 1. Election Bribery Allegation (2022)
On April 22, 2022, Wilson emailed district staff regarding the May 10 bond issue election: *"If 100% of the eligible voters in your building vote before May 10th we will bring lunch for you one day before the end of school...The first building in our district to get 100% also gets dessert with their lunch!"*

This was reported as potential **election bribery** under Kansas law. Ellis County Attorney Robert Anderson declined to prosecute, saying he could not "justify prosecuting" because it would divert resources from "sexual assaults, homicides, child abuse and neglect" and other serious cases. **The county attorney did NOT say it wasn't a violation -- he said prosecution wasn't worth the resources.** The bond passed (~$143.5M).

### 3. Academic Performance Concerns
Per Kansas Sentinel (March 2023): USD 489 had **no apparent written plan to improve low proficiency levels**. Key findings:
- A quarter of students are below grade level in math
- 52% of low-income students proficient in math in 3rd grade but only **8% proficient by 10th grade**
- The district did not publish building-level reports as required by state law
- More recent data shows improvement: district scores trending above state average in both math and English
- District earned a Silver Award for graduation rate (93.5% in 2024, up from 89.6% in 2023)
- 6 state accreditation honors for 2022-23

### 4. Trump Administration Federal Funds Withheld (2025)
The Trump administration froze **$172,000 in federal title funds** for USD 489. Wilson stated the funds were already allocated and would be distributed after 45 days unless Congress voted to claw back. Title funds support reading/math instruction, migrant student support, and professional development. Wilson noted Kansas had already eliminated state funding for professional development.

Source: [Trump administration withholds funds from Hays school district](https://hayspost.com/posts/09e13f5d-3612-4ec5-9106-d99396d14ac3)

Parents complained about the potential for opposite-sex students being "intimate" in stalls, and about vaping. Wilson as superintendent oversees the facility design decisions.

- **Special Education:** $12M/year
- **Capital Outlay 5-Year:** $6.4M (2024-25), $5.3M (2025-26), $5.5M (2026-27)

## Teacher Pay
- **Average Teacher Salary:** $67,857 (per government salary data)
- **Kansas State Average:** $50,989-$56,481 (varies by source)
- **USD 489 Premium:** Roughly $11,000-$17,000 ABOVE Kansas state average
- **Recent Raises:** $1,910 added to each cell of teacher salary schedule for 2024-25

## Bond & Construction
- **Bond:** $143.5M passed May 10, 2022 (3,239 for, 2,773 against; 53.9% approval)
- **New Hays High School:** $115M, opened August 20, 2025. Includes performing arts center, athletic fields, art studios, journalism/video labs, culinary arts kitchen, collaborative spaces. Architect: DLR Group. Construction Manager: Nabholz Construction.
- **New Hays Middle School:** Former high school converted. Renovation came in $300K under budget ($1.925M max guaranteed price). Students moved in January 2026. Classrooms expanded from 700 to 900 sq ft.
- **Roosevelt Elementary Expansion:** $21.9M addition/renovation completed December 2024. Capacity expanded from 3 to 5 classes per grade (690 students). New media center, gym/storm shelter.
- **0.5% Sales Tax:** Approved May 10, 2022 by Hays voters. Collection started October 1, 2022. Expires after 10 years (October 2032). Reduces property tax requirement of the 30-year bond. Hays receives NO state aid for bond/capital improvements.
