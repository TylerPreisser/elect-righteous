# Election Finder -- Soul

## What I've Learned
*Updated after Run #1 — 2026-03-30 — Hays, Kansas (Ellis County)*

### Kansas-Specific Knowledge

**Election Cycle Structure:**
- Kansas holds partisan primaries on the first Tuesday in August of even years.
- General elections are the first Tuesday after the first Monday in November.
- ALL 125 Kansas House seats are up every 2 years (2026, 2028, etc.).
- ALL 40 Kansas State Senate seats were elected in 2024, next up in 2028. No Senate races in 2026.
- Municipal elections (city commissions) are in ODD-numbered years (November).
- School board elections are in ODD-numbered years (November), non-partisan.
- State Board of Education: even-numbered districts in presidential years, odd-numbered in midterms.
- Filing deadline for state/federal offices: June 1 at noon.

**Statewide Offices on 4-Year Cycle (All Up in 2026):**
- Governor/Lt. Governor
- Attorney General
- Secretary of State
- State Treasurer
- Insurance Commissioner
- State Board of Education (5 of 10 seats -- odd-numbered districts)

**District Mapping for Hays/Ellis County:**
- U.S. House: KS-01 ("The Big First") -- covers western/central Kansas
- Kansas State Senate: District 40 (NOT up in 2026)
- Kansas State House: District 111 (Barb Wasinger, R)
- Kansas SBOE: District 5 (Cathy Hopkins, R)
- Judicial District: 23rd (Ellis, Gove, Sheridan, Trego counties)

### Search Effectiveness

**Best Sources (Ranked):**
1. **Ballotpedia** -- Most comprehensive, best-structured data. Has candidate lists, election timelines, and district maps. Start here ALWAYS.
2. **Wikipedia election pages** -- Good for candidate lists and background, especially for major races. Often more detailed than Ballotpedia on candidate bios.
3. **KCUR (Kansas City NPR)** -- Excellent candidate guides with detailed profiles. Published comprehensive guides for governor and U.S. Senate races.
4. **Kansas Reflector** -- Best for in-depth Kansas political journalism. Covers debates, policy analysis, legislative votes.
5. **Kansas Secretary of State (sos.ks.gov)** -- Official dates and filing information. Election calendar is authoritative.
6. **FEC.gov** -- Federal race filing data. Useful for confirming who has filed for U.S. House and Senate.
7. **Kansas Legislature website (kslegislature.gov)** -- Official district maps, legislator rosters, voting records.
8. **KSDE.gov** -- State Board of Education district information and county assignments.
9. **Ellis County website (ellisco.net)** -- Local election information, candidate filings, election results.
10. **Hays Post (hayspost.com)** -- Local Hays news. Covers city commission and school board races.

**Sources That Didn't Work Well:**
- Vote.org -- Too generic, doesn't have Kansas-specific upcoming election details.
- Vote Smart -- Limited current information for 2026 cycle at time of search.
- Local newspaper paywalls limited some research.

### Common Pitfalls

1. **August ballot measures are easy to miss.** The Supreme Court election amendment is on the AUGUST PRIMARY ballot, not the general. This is a huge deal -- 2022 abortion amendment was also on a primary ballot and drove massive turnout.

2. **Odd/even year confusion.** Municipal and school board elections in Kansas are in ODD years (2025, 2027). Do NOT expect them on the 2026 ballot. Confirm cycle before listing.

3. **Open seats vs. incumbent races.** In 2026, Governor, Secretary of State, and Insurance Commissioner are ALL open seats because incumbents are either term-limited or running for other offices. This dramatically changes the race dynamics.

4. **Kansas Senate is NOT up in 2026.** All 40 seats were elected in 2024 for 4-year terms. Next up in 2028. Do not confuse with U.S. Senate (which IS on the 2026 ballot).

5. **Judicial retention is invisible.** These races appear at the bottom of the ballot and most voters skip them, but they have real consequences. The specific judges up for retention are hard to find until closer to the election -- must verify through Kansas Bar Association or SOS.

6. **County commission races need direct verification.** Online sources don't always list county-level races clearly. The Ellis County Clerk (785-628-9410) is the authoritative source.

7. **SBOE district assignments are not intuitive.** Ellis County is in SBOE District 5 (western Kansas), not District 7. Must check KSDE.gov county-by-district mapping.

8. **Community college and special district elections** (water, hospital, library boards) are extremely hard to find online. These typically require direct contact with county election offices.
