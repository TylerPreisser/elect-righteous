# Judeo-Christian Values Assessment Framework

## Purpose

This framework provides a structured, fair, and evidence-based way to assess how a candidate's PUBLIC RECORD aligns with Judeo-Christian moral principles. This is NOT about judging a person's faith — it's about measuring observable actions and positions against a consistent moral standard.

## Important Disclaimers

1. **This is not a salvation test** — No one is perfect. This framework measures public record, not the state of someone's soul.
2. **Actions over words** — Saying "I'm a Christian" means nothing without evidence in action. Conversely, someone who never claims faith may demonstrate these values perfectly.
3. **Context matters** — A single vote or statement is rarely enough to assess alignment. Look for patterns.
4. **Grace required** — People change, grow, and repent. Historical positions that were acknowledged and corrected should be weighted differently than ongoing patterns.

## The Eight Pillars

### 1. Sanctity of Life

**Biblical Basis**: "Before I formed you in the womb I knew you" (Jeremiah 1:5); "You shall not murder" (Exodus 20:13)

**What to Assess**:
- Position on abortion (stated and voted)
- Position on euthanasia/assisted suicide
- Position on death penalty
- Positions on healthcare access
- Positions affecting mortality (poverty, war, environmental health)
- **Consistency**: Are they pro-life across ALL life issues, or selectively?

**Rating Guide**:
- **Strong**: Consistent defense of life across multiple issues with legislative/action record
- **Moderate**: Generally supportive with some inconsistencies
- **Weak**: Positions that undermine the sanctity of life
- **Unknown**: Insufficient public record

### 2. Protection of Family

**Biblical Basis**: "Honor your father and your mother" (Exodus 20:12); "Train up a child in the way he should go" (Proverbs 22:6)

**What to Assess**:
- Support for parental rights in education
- Positions on marriage and family policy
- Support for families in economic policy
- Positions on child welfare and protection
- Their own family conduct (only what's public and relevant to public trust)

**Rating Guide**:
- **Strong**: Active support for family-strengthening policy with demonstrated personal commitment
- **Moderate**: Generally supportive without strong record
- **Weak**: Positions that undermine family stability
- **Unknown**: Insufficient public record

### 3. Religious Liberty

**Biblical Basis**: "Give to Caesar what is Caesar's, and to God what is God's" (Matthew 22:21); "Where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is freedom" (2 Corinthians 3:17)

**What to Assess**:
- Defense of First Amendment religious freedom
- Support for religious expression in public life
- Respect for conscience protections
- Treatment of people of different faiths
- Balance between religious liberty and other rights

**Rating Guide**:
- **Strong**: Active defense of religious liberty for all faiths
- **Moderate**: Generally supportive without strong advocacy
- **Weak**: Positions that restrict religious expression
- **Unknown**: Insufficient public record

### 4. Honesty & Integrity

**Biblical Basis**: "You shall not bear false witness" (Exodus 20:16); "The Lord detests lying lips, but he delights in people who are trustworthy" (Proverbs 12:22)

**What to Assess**:
- Track record of truthful public statements
- Transparency in governance/business
- Willingness to admit mistakes publicly
- Consistency between public and private behavior
- Campaign honesty (do ads/claims check out?)

**Rating Guide**:
- **Strong**: Demonstrable pattern of honesty, even when costly
- **Moderate**: Generally honest with minor inconsistencies
- **Weak**: Pattern of deception, misleading statements, or verifiable lies
- **Unknown**: Insufficient public record

### 5. Care for the Vulnerable

**Biblical Basis**: "Whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers and sisters of mine, you did for me" (Matthew 25:40); "Defend the weak and the fatherless; uphold the cause of the poor and the oppressed" (Psalm 82:3)

**What to Assess**:
- Positions on poverty, homelessness, hunger
- Support for mental health and addiction services
- Care for elderly and disabled
- Refugee and immigrant treatment
- Education access for underserved communities
- Personal charitable involvement (not just political positions)

**Rating Guide**:
- **Strong**: Active policy support AND personal involvement in serving the vulnerable
- **Moderate**: Some policy support or personal involvement
- **Weak**: Positions that harm vulnerable populations
- **Unknown**: Insufficient public record

### 6. Justice & Rule of Law

**Biblical Basis**: "He has told you, O man, what is good; and what does the Lord require of you but to do justice, and to love kindness, and to walk humbly with your God?" (Micah 6:8); "Do not pervert justice; do not show partiality to the poor or favoritism to the great, but judge your neighbor fairly" (Leviticus 19:15)

**What to Assess**:
- Respect for constitutional processes and rule of law
- Equal application of rules (no favoritism)
- Response to corruption or misconduct (their own and others')
- Support for fair judicial processes
- Criminal justice reform positions

**Rating Guide**:
- **Strong**: Consistent commitment to equal justice and constitutional order
- **Moderate**: Generally supportive with some selective application
- **Weak**: Willingness to bend rules for political gain
- **Unknown**: Insufficient public record

### 7. Stewardship of Resources

**Biblical Basis**: "The earth is the Lord's, and everything in it" (Psalm 24:1); "Whoever can be trusted with very little can also be trusted with much" (Luke 16:10)

**What to Assess**:
- Fiscal responsibility with public money
- Environmental stewardship
- Responsible use of authority and resources
- Long-term thinking vs. short-term gain
- Personal financial responsibility (where publicly relevant)

**Rating Guide**:
- **Strong**: Demonstrated responsible management of resources, both fiscal and environmental
- **Moderate**: Generally responsible with some concerns
- **Weak**: Wasteful, exploitative, or irresponsible with entrusted resources
- **Unknown**: Insufficient public record

### 8. Community & Neighborly Love

**Biblical Basis**: "Love your neighbor as yourself" (Mark 12:31); "If it is possible, as far as it depends on you, live at peace with everyone" (Romans 12:18)

**What to Assess**:
- Unifying vs. divisive rhetoric and actions
- Treatment of political opponents
- Civic engagement beyond self-interest
- Bridge-building across differences
- Respectful discourse, even in disagreement

**Rating Guide**:
- **Strong**: Active bridge-builder who treats all with dignity, even opponents
- **Moderate**: Generally respectful with occasional partisan divisiveness
- **Weak**: Primarily divisive, demonizes opponents, tears down community
- **Unknown**: Insufficient public record

---

## Scoring Rules

1. **Evidence-based only** — Every rating must cite specific public evidence
2. **Pattern over incident** — One bad day doesn't define someone; a pattern does
3. **Weight recent more** — Recent record matters more than distant past
4. **Acknowledge change** — If someone genuinely changed a position, note both the old and new
5. **N/A is valid** — If there's no evidence, say "Unknown" — don't guess
6. **Both sides of every coin** — If they're strong on one aspect of a value but weak on another, note both
7. **No perfection expected** — "Strong" doesn't mean perfect. It means a clear, consistent pattern
