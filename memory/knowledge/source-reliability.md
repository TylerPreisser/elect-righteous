# Source Reliability Ratings

## Tier 1: Primary / Official Sources (Highest Trust)
- Official government websites (.gov)
- Court records (PACER, state court systems)
- Campaign finance filings (FEC, state ethics commission)
- Official meeting minutes (city, county, school board)
- Legislative voting records
- Property and business filing records
- **Treatment**: Facts from these sources can be stated directly

## Tier 2: Established Journalism (High Trust)
- Major Kansas newspapers (Wichita Eagle, Topeka Capital-Journal, Kansas City Star)
- Local newspapers (Hays Daily News, Hays Post, Salina Journal)
- Kansas TV stations (KWCH, KSNW, KAKE)
- AP, Reuters, major wire services
- Ballotpedia (well-sourced entries)
- **Treatment**: Generally trustworthy but should be corroborated when possible

## Tier 3: Candidate's Own Statements (Medium Trust)
- Campaign websites
- Social media posts (verified accounts)
- Interview transcripts (from Tier 2 sources)
- Press releases
- **Treatment**: Trustworthy as evidence of what the candidate SAYS, but claims within must be independently verified

## Tier 4: Community & Analysis Sources (Lower Trust, Higher Context)
- OpenSecrets.org, FollowTheMoney.org (data is solid, analysis may lean)
- Vote Smart (generally reliable, may have gaps)
- Local blogs and community forums
- Nextdoor posts
- Business review sites (Google Reviews, Glassdoor, Yelp)
- **Treatment**: Useful for community sentiment and leads, but claims must be verified against Tier 1-2 sources

## Tier 5: Social Media & Informal (Lowest Individual Trust, Pattern Value)
- Reddit posts and comments
- Facebook group discussions
- Twitter/X threads
- Anonymous online comments
- Political advocacy sites (from any direction)
- **Treatment**: Never cite as sole source for a factual claim. Useful for identifying leads to investigate and understanding public sentiment. Multiple independent accounts saying the same thing adds weight.

## Reliability Rules

1. **Two-source rule**: Any significant claim should be verified by at least two independent sources
2. **Primary trumps secondary**: Official records beat news reports beat social media
3. **Recency matters**: More recent sources are more reliable for current positions
4. **Bias awareness**: Note when a source has an obvious political lean (this applies to sources on ALL sides)
5. **Archive for permanence**: Web content can be deleted — note access dates and archive when possible
6. **Absence ≠ innocence**: Not finding negative information doesn't mean it doesn't exist
7. **Absence ≠ guilt**: Not finding information doesn't mean there's a cover-up
