# Kansas Election Law Reference

## Key Resources
- Kansas Secretary of State Elections Division: https://sos.ks.gov/elections/
- Kansas Governmental Ethics Commission: https://ethics.kansas.gov/
- Kansas Legislature: http://www.kslegislature.org/

## Election Calendar (General)
- **Primary Elections**: First Tuesday in August (even years for partisan, odd years for some local)
- **General Elections**: First Tuesday after first Monday in November
- **Local/School Elections**: Varies — many Kansas cities and school districts hold elections in odd years (November) or spring
- **Special Elections**: Called as needed by Governor or local authority

## Filing Requirements
- **State offices**: File with Secretary of State
- **County offices**: File with County Election Officer
- **City offices**: File with City Clerk
- **School board**: File with County Election Officer

## Campaign Finance
- **State-level**: Report to Kansas Governmental Ethics Commission
- **Federal**: Report to FEC
- **Local**: Varies by jurisdiction — check city/county requirements
- **Reporting thresholds**: Kansas requires campaign finance reports for candidates raising/spending above certain thresholds

## Kansas-Specific Notes
- Kansas is a **closed primary** state (must be registered with party to vote in primary)
- **Voter registration deadline**: 21 days before election
- **Advance voting**: Available at county election offices
- **Ellis County Election Office**: County Clerk handles elections
- **Hays, Kansas**: City of the first class, commission-manager form of government

## Useful Databases
