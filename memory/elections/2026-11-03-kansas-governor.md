# Kansas Governor & Lieutenant Governor Election, 2026

## Key Dates
- **Filing Deadline**: 2026-06-01 (noon)
- **Primary**: 2026-08-04
- **General Election**: 2026-11-03
- **Voter Registration Deadline (Primary)**: 2026-07-14
- **Voter Registration Deadline (General)**: 2026-10-13

## Position(s)
- Governor of Kansas + Lieutenant Governor (joint ticket) -- 1 seat
- Current incumbent: Laura Kelly (D) -- **term-limited, cannot run again**
- Term length: 4 years

## Jurisdiction
- **Level**: State
- **District/Ward**: Statewide
- **Eligible Voters**: All registered Kansas voters

## Declared Candidates

### Republican Primary
1. **Scott Schwab** -- Current Kansas Secretary of State (term-limited). First to announce. Known for pushing back against election conspiracy theories. From Overland Park.
2. **Vicki Schmidt** -- Current Kansas Insurance Commissioner. Former state senator (2005-2019). Leaving Insurance Commissioner seat open.
3. **Ty Masterson** -- President of the Kansas State Senate. State senator since 2009 from eastern Kansas. Small-business owner.
4. **Jeff Colyer** -- Former Governor of Kansas (succeeded Sam Brownback 2018, lost 2018 primary to Kobach). Physician.
5. **Charlotte O'Hara** -- Former Johnson County Commissioner.
6. **Stacy Rogers** -- Wichita-based business owner.
7. **Ron Estes** -- U.S. Representative (KS-04, Wichita area). Exploring/declared.
8. **Jake LaTurner** -- U.S. Representative (KS-02, eastern Kansas). Exploring/declared.
9. **Dayton Moore** -- Former Kansas City Royals executive. Exploring.
10. **Mark Steffen** -- Former state senator.

### Democratic Primary
1. **Ethan Corson** -- State Senator from Prairie Village (Johnson County). First Democrat elected from his Senate district. Former executive director of Kansas Democratic Party. Former roles at International Trade Administration and U.S. Department of Commerce.
2. **Cindy Holscher** -- State Senator from Overland Park. Served two terms each in House and Senate since 2021. First-generation college graduate.
3. **Marty Tuley** -- From Lawrence. Limited public profile information available.

## Notes
- This is a WIDE-OPEN race with no incumbent. Laura Kelly (D) is term-limited.
- The Republican primary is extremely crowded (8-10 candidates) and will be the most competitive race on the ballot.
- The six-candidate GOP debate in January 2026 suggests a highly competitive primary.
- Kansas has elected governors from both parties recently: Kelly (D, 2018, 2022), Brownback/Colyer (R, 2010, 2014).
- The winner will have significant power over Kansas policy including Medicaid expansion (which Kelly championed but legislature blocked).
