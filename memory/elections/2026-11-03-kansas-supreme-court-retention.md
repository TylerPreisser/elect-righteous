# Kansas Supreme Court Retention Elections

## Election Details
- **Date**: November 3, 2026 (General Election)
- **Type**: Judicial Retention (Yes/No vote)
- **Jurisdiction**: Statewide (all Kansas voters including Hays/Ellis County)

## Justices Up for Retention

### Justice Eric Rosen (Chief Justice)
- **Position**: Chief Justice, Kansas Supreme Court
- **Appointed By**: Democratic Governor Kathleen Sebelius (2005)
- **Term Expires**: January 11, 2027
- **Retention Cycle**: Every 6 years
- **Ballot Question**: "Shall Justice Eric Rosen be retained in office?" YES / NO

### Justice Larkin Walsh
- **Position**: Justice, Kansas Supreme Court
- **Appointed By**: Democratic Governor Laura Kelly (August 7, 2025)
- **Sworn In**: September 17, 2025
- **Replaced**: Justice Evelyn Z. Wilson (retired July 4, 2025)
- **Term Expires**: January 11, 2027
- **Note**: This is Walsh's first retention vote (newly appointed judges must stand for retention in the next even-year general election after serving at least one year)
- **Ballot Question**: "Shall Justice Larkin Walsh be retained in office?" YES / NO

## How It Works
- Kansas Supreme Court justices are appointed by the Governor from a list provided by the Supreme Court Nominating Commission
- After appointment, justices must stand for retention in the next even-year general election
- If retained, they serve 6-year terms and face retention every 6 years
- A simple majority "No" vote removes the justice from office
- Justices may serve until age 75

## Court Composition (2026)
- 5 of 7 justices were appointed by Democratic governors
- 2 of 7 justices were appointed by Republican governors

## Important Context
- If the August 2026 constitutional amendment passes, these may be the LAST retention elections for Kansas Supreme Court justices
- The system would transition to partisan elections starting in 2028
