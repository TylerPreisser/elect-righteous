## Notes
- The State Board of Education is composed of 10 members elected from districts of 4 contiguous state senate districts each.
- Elections are staggered: even-numbered districts in presidential years, odd-numbered districts in midterms.
- The SBOE sets education policy, approves curriculum standards, and oversees the Kansas Department of Education.
- The 2024 SBOE elections shifted the board toward a conservative majority. The 2026 races could continue or reverse that trend.
- District 5 is a vast rural district -- candidates need to campaign across dozens of counties.
- No candidates filed yet, but June 1 deadline means this race is still forming.
