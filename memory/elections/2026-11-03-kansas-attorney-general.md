## Position(s)
- Kansas Attorney General -- 1 seat
- Current incumbent: **Kris Kobach (R)** -- elected 2022 (narrowly, by 1.6 points / ~15,000 votes over 1 million cast)
- Term length: 4 years

## Jurisdiction
- **Level**: State
- **District/Ward**: Statewide
- **Eligible Voters**: All registered Kansas voters

## Declared Candidates

### Republican Primary
1. **Kris Kobach** (incumbent) -- Seeking second term. Former Kansas Secretary of State (2011-2019). Failed gubernatorial candidate (2018). Known nationally for immigration enforcement and voter fraud investigations. Has announced re-election campaign.

### Democratic Primary
1. **Chris Mann** -- Lawrence attorney and former police officer. His law enforcement career was cut short when struck by a DUI motorist. Lost to Kobach in 2022 by only 1.6 percentage points (~15,000 votes) -- the closest statewide margin that cycle. Seeking rematch.

## Notes
- This is one of the MOST COMPETITIVE statewide races. The 2022 race was decided by only 1.6 points.
- Kobach is a nationally polarizing figure -- strong support from conservative base, strong opposition from moderates/Democrats.
- Chris Mann is positioning for a rematch. His law enforcement background gives him credibility on public safety issues that could appeal to moderate Republicans.
- The AG race could be significantly affected by turnout driven by the governor's race and ballot measures.
- Kobach has used the AG office to pursue high-profile litigation on immigration, transgender policies, and other conservative causes.
