# Kansas Citizenship Voting Requirement Constitutional Amendment (November 2026)

## Key Dates
- **Election Date**: 2026-11-03 (on the GENERAL election ballot)
- **Voter Registration Deadline**: 2026-10-13

## Position(s)
- Constitutional Amendment -- Yes/No vote
- This is NOT a candidate race. This is a ballot measure.
- Legislatively referred constitutional amendment

## Jurisdiction
- **Level**: State -- Constitutional Amendment
- **District/Ward**: Statewide
- **Eligible Voters**: All registered Kansas voters

## What the Amendment Would Do
- Amend Section 1 of Article 5 of the Kansas Constitution
- Specify that in order to vote in Kansas, individuals must be:
  1. Citizens (of the United States)
  2. At least 18 years old
  3. A resident of the area in which the person is seeking to vote

## Context
- Similar citizenship voting requirement amendments were approved in 8 states in 2024
- Non-citizens are already prohibited from voting in Kansas and federal elections by existing law
- Proponents argue this enshrines the requirement in the state constitution as an additional safeguard
- Opponents argue it is unnecessary and could be used to create additional barriers to voting

- This is widely expected to pass, as similar measures passed in all 8 states where they appeared in 2024.
- The practical impact is debated -- non-citizens are already prohibited from voting under existing state and federal law.
- The political significance may be more symbolic than practical, but it could affect voter registration procedures and enforcement mechanisms depending on implementing legislation.
