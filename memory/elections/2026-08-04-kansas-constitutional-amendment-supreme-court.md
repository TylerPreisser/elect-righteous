# Kansas Elections for Supreme Court Justices Amendment

## Election Details
- **Date**: August 4, 2026 (Primary Election ballot)
- **Type**: Constitutional Amendment (legislatively referred)
- **Jurisdiction**: Statewide (all Kansas voters)
- **Ballot Question**: Whether to amend the Kansas Constitution to provide for direct partisan election of Kansas Supreme Court justices, abolishing the existing Supreme Court Nominating Commission

## What It Would Do
- Replace the current merit-selection/retention system with direct partisan elections for Supreme Court justices
- Abolish the Kansas Supreme Court Nominating Commission
- Justices' terms would remain at six years
- Vacancies would be filled by election as provided by law
- If approved: Positions 1, 2, 3 elected in 2028; Positions 4, 5 in 2030; Positions 6, 7 in 2032

## Passage Requirement
- Simple majority of votes cast on the question

## Background
- SCR 1611 passed the Kansas House and Senate in March 2025
- Placed on the August 2026 primary ballot (not November general)
- Supported by Republican lawmakers who argue it makes justices more accountable
- Opposed by Democratic legislators and legal professionals who argue it invites partisan politics into the judiciary

## Confidence Level
HIGH - Confirmed via multiple sources (Ballotpedia, Kansas Reflector, KCUR, Kansas Legislature)
