## Public Platform Signals

Rogers' current campaign site uses a direct outsider-and-service frame: "People Over Politics. Kansas First." It says she wants a stronger future for every Kansas community, describes the Rogers-Smith ticket as first to formally enter the 2026 governor's race, and frames the campaign around courage, grassroots leadership, listening to Kansans, fiscal responsibility, small business, public safety, rural values, agriculture, and government that works for people rather than insiders. ([source](https://stacyrogersforks.com/))

The Meet Stacy page is the clearest candidate-controlled issue summary. It identifies Rogers as a lifelong Kansan, Republican, business owner, mother, and non-career politician. It lists priorities including sunsetting STAR bonds, returning ALBTR cigarette and alcohol tax revenue to counties, protecting against predatory energy projects, encouraging homegrown business, service above politics, and people above special interests. Its core-values list includes pro-Second Amendment, pro-business, pro-veteran, pro-family, natural and constitutional rights, law enforcement, and term limits. ([source](https://stacyrogersforks.com/meet-stacy))

The Rogers Report page is positioned as a candidate-controlled update channel with campaign updates, event announcements, video messages, press releases, policy positions, and "behind-the-scenes" material. It explicitly brands itself as "Straight Talk. Real Issues. For All Kansans." On capture, the visible post inventory was thin: the page exposed a "Business & Beards Podcast - Stacy Rogers Candidate Gov" heading but did not provide a full accessible archive of posts. ([source](https://stacyrogersforks.com/the-rogers-report))

The campaign Linktree confirms the cross-platform footprint and paid-for disclaimer. It links donation support, the campaign website, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, and X. The current campaign website also lists Truth Social, Instagram, YouTube, Facebook/Twitter handle `@StacyRogersKS`, and a general cross-platform handle `@StacyRogersforKS`. ([Linktree source](https://linktr.ee/stacyrogersforkansas), [campaign source](https://stacyrogersforks.com/meet-stacy))

LinkedIn was the highest visible engagement/presence signal in the logged-out capture set. The public LinkedIn profile listed roughly 2K followers and 500+ connections, with the profile name "Stacy Rogers For Governor, (Archie Macias, Treasurer)." It also included older business links and an About line saying, "To honor God in all that I do." Visible activity snippets included a post asking to add volunteers to a Facebook chat for shirts/hats and walking/calling, a share saying she "cannot be bought," and a shared county-travel schedule for Smith, Jewell, Mitchell, Lincoln, Osborne, and Russell counties. ([source](https://www.linkedin.com/in/stacy-rogers-for-kansas))

Indexed X metadata identified [@stacyrogersks](https://x.com/stacyrogersks) as "Stacy Rogers for Kansas Governor," Kansas, USA, with the campaign website and paid-for disclaimer. TwStalker snippets showed follower counts around 546 and following around 2K in one indexed view, while another older indexed view showed 357 followers and 2K following. Because X itself did not expose reliable logged-out profile counts in this capture, those counts are treated as indexed estimates, not current official numbers. ([indexed source](https://twstalker.com/Geebu55288))

Indexed TikTok and YouTube metadata surfaced modest public followings: `@stacyrogersforthepeople` was indexed with 718 TikTok followers and a campaign disclaimer, while `@StacyRogersForThePeople` was indexed with 66 YouTube subscribers and a campaign disclaimer. No reliable logged-out post-level engagement was visible from either native platform. ([source](https://www.idcrawl.com/stacy-rogers))

The richest long-form candidate voice came from podcast appearances rather than platform-native posts. On the Billy Dees Podcast, Rogers said she is on "Snapchat," TikTok, Rumble, YouTube, Truth Social, Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and her website, and said she personally answers text messages, Facebook messages, and email. The same transcript covered special interests, fentanyl and human trafficking, education, foster care, term limits, and Kansas Supreme Court selection. ([source](https://www.iheart.com/podcast/966-billy-dees-podcast-28826157/episode/stacy-rogers-candidate-for-governor-280748522/))

## Highest Visible Engagement

The highest visible public presence metric in the capture set was LinkedIn: roughly 2K followers and 500+ connections on the campaign profile. Indexed TikTok metadata showed 718 followers; indexed X metadata showed follower counts in the mid-hundreds; indexed YouTube metadata showed 66 subscribers. No reliable public page-level likes or post-level engagement were visible for the campaign Facebook page in the logged-out capture path.

No reliable post-level engagement was visible from the native Facebook, X, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Truth Social, Rumble, or Snapchat feeds. Engagement numbers above are account-level or indexed-profile estimates only.

## Repost / Amplification Review

The accessible LinkedIn activity snippets showed a mix of candidate posts, shares, and likes: volunteer recruitment through a Facebook chat, a campaign share saying she "cannot be bought," a shared county-travel schedule, and likes on other users' business/community posts. The campaign site embeds or references podcast/video-style content, including Cowley County and Business & Beards/Rogers Report material. Because native platform feeds were login- or script-limited, no complete repost/share inventory is reported.

## Visible Absences

Across the accessible public set reviewed, no reviewed item centered Medicaid expansion, water policy or the Ogallala aquifer, higher-education affordability, housing affordability, child care affordability as a standalone issue, road and bridge funding, broadband, labor unions, state employee pay, marijuana legalization, LGBTQ+ policy, or detailed abortion exceptions. The site includes pro-life/family framing and broad constitutional-rights language, but it does not provide detailed policy papers on most of those topics in the accessible pages reviewed.

The campaign publicly claims or links a wide platform footprint, but several accounts could not be verified at post level in a logged-out capture. Rumble and Snapchat were claimed by Rogers in a podcast transcript but no public account URLs were verified. Bluesky, Threads, Gab, Gettr, Substack, and Reddit did not surface as candidate-controlled channels.

## Methodology

Date range covered: 2024-11-06 to 2026-05-11 for campaign launch/social evidence and current platform presence; current capture performed on 2026-05-11. Platforms surveyed: campaign website, Linktree, Facebook, X, LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Truth Social, Rumble, Snapchat, Bluesky, Threads, Gab, Gettr, Substack, Reddit, public podcast platforms, indexed public-profile sources, and local news/indexed public posts.

Items examined: 16 accessible campaign/profile/public-record/news/podcast items and 0 complete verified repost inventories from platform-native feeds. Login-walled, script-blocked, temporarily blocked, or otherwise inaccessible social feeds were marked as limited and not counted as post-level reviewed. Engagement was reported only where visible in public metadata, indexed profile snippets, or logged-out page modules on capture date.

Disclaimer: This is a public, logged-out harvest of candidate-controlled public channels plus credible indexed public posts and public podcast/news sources through 2026-05-11. It reports visible patterns and documented absences in the capture set; it does not infer intent, private strategy, deleted content, algorithmic reach, or unobserved activity.
