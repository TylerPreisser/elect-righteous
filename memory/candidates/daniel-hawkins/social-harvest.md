Direct engagement counts on X, Facebook, and Instagram were not consistently public because X, Facebook, and Instagram pages were login-walled or script-blocked in this environment. The highest visible reach/engagement signals were therefore indirect:

- X profile scale: indexed Twitter profile preview listed [@DanHawkinsKS](https://x.com/DanHawkinsKS) at about 2,000 followers and 339 following.
- Facebook feed visibility: [danhawkinskansas.com](https://danhawkinskansas.com/) embeds recent posts from "Speaker Dan Hawkins," making Facebook the richest public social feed surfaced without login.
- YouTube scale: indexed YouTube preview for [@vote4hawkins](https://www.youtube.com/@vote4hawkins) showed about 57-58 subscribers, suggesting a minor channel compared with X/Facebook.
- News amplification: the launch message was picked up by [Kansas Reflector](https://kansasreflector.com/2025/05/13/kansas-house-speaker-launches-campaign-for-insurance-commissioner-with-pro-trump-rhetoric-1/); later statements were republished or quoted by outlets including [WIBW](https://www.wibw.com/2026/04/27/gov-kelly-vetoes-property-tax-protest-bill-kansas-house-speaker-responds/) and [HPPR/Kansas Reflector](https://www.hppr.org/hppr-news/2026-04-20/food-assistance-audit-finds-potential-1-2-million-paid-to-people-living-outside-kansas?_amp=true).

## Visible Absences And Limits

- X direct profile opened to a JavaScript/blocked shell in this browser, so individual tweet engagement counts were not harvested directly.
- Facebook direct page content was not fully accessible without platform rendering/login, but the older campaign site exposes a public embedded feed.
- Instagram direct content was not publicly readable here; only the account link and indexed account identity were confirmed.
- YouTube channel exists but current videos and channel metadata were not fully readable through direct open; indexed preview showed only a small subscriber count and political-page description.
- No obvious campaign-controlled TikTok, Threads, Bluesky, or LinkedIn account was verified.
- I did not use private, deleted, login-only, or paywalled social content.

## Methodology

I checked the candidate's current campaign site, older campaign site, official Kansas Legislature page, linked social icons, search-indexed social previews, public X mirrors, public news articles embedding or quoting @DanHawkinsKS posts, and public video/interview pages. I treated website copy, embedded public Facebook-feed text, public X snippets, and news-quoted candidate statements as candidate-controlled or candidate-attributed material. I excluded opponent commentary unless it directly reproduced Hawkins' words or account metadata. Engagement observations are limited to visible public counts and public amplification because platform pages were partly login-walled or script-blocked.
