## Public Platform Signals

The campaign website frames Schmidt as a lifelong Kansan, Republican, pharmacist, former state senator, breast-cancer survivor, and sitting insurance commissioner. Its lead line says, "It's time Kansans have a governor that works as hard as they do." It emphasizes finding Medicaid drug-rebate errors that led to more than $391 million recouped for the state, autism coverage, cancer-care access, more than $206 million returned to Kansas families as insurance commissioner, and more than $96 million in lowered cost of doing business. ([source](https://vickiforgovernor.com/))

The campaign site links only Facebook and X in the visible header/footer social links. It does not visibly link Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Bluesky, Truth Social, Gab, Gettr, Substack, or Reddit. ([source](https://vickiforgovernor.com/))

The campaign Facebook page metadata captured publicly on 2026-05-11 listed 3,403 likes and 1,496 talking about this. Its visible description identified Schmidt as a lifelong Kansan and Republican, mother and grandmother, local pharmacist, and running for governor, with a paid-for-by disclaimer for Vicki Schmidt for Governor. ([source](https://www.facebook.com/VickiSchmidtKS))

Public X profile metadata was not returned directly by X in logged-out capture, but indexed public profile snippets identified [@VickiSchmidtKS](https://x.com/VickiSchmidtKS) as having roughly 2K followers and 1K following, with the same campaign bio: lifelong Kansan and Republican, mother and grandmother, local pharmacist, running for governor. ([source](https://w.twstalker.com/MaysAaron))

Kansas.gov's social-media directory lists Insurance Commissioner Vicki Schmidt with official Facebook and Twitter/X links. The Department's website footer also links official Instagram, Facebook, X, and LinkedIn accounts. ([Kansas.gov source](https://portal.kansas.gov/social-media/), [Department source](https://www.insurance.kansas.gov/Home/Components/News/News/226/16))

The office Facebook page metadata captured publicly on 2026-05-11 listed 1,960 likes, 33 talking about this, and 115 check-ins for "Kansas Insurance Department, Topeka." ([source](https://www.facebook.com/KSinsurancedept/))

The office Instagram profile metadata captured publicly on 2026-05-11 listed 21 followers, 62 following, and 67 posts for [@kansasdoi](https://www.instagram.com/kansasdoi/), with the bio line: "Serving Kansans with the mission to Regulate, Educate, and Advocate since 1871." ([source](https://www.instagram.com/kansasdoi/))

The office LinkedIn page was the richest accessible social feed. It listed 623 followers and a government-administration profile for the Kansas Department of Insurance. Visible updates emphasized hiring, fraud and scam prevention, PBM reform, National Agriculture Day, NAIC participation, financial education, storm-damage claims, and consumer-protection resources. ([source](https://www.linkedin.com/company/kansas-department-of-insurance))

## Methodology

Date range covered: 2019-02-18 to 2026-05-11 for indexed public social posts/statements and current platform presence; current capture performed on 2026-05-11. Platforms surveyed: campaign website, campaign Facebook, campaign X, official Kansas.gov social directory, office Facebook, office X, office Instagram, office LinkedIn, personal LinkedIn URL, YouTube, Bluesky, TikTok, Truth Social, Gab, Gettr, Substack, and Reddit.

Items examined: 17 accessible campaign/profile/public-record/news items and 0 verified reposts from platform-native feeds. Login-walled, script-blocked, or otherwise inaccessible social feeds were marked as limited and not counted as post-level reviewed. Engagement was reported only where visible in public metadata or logged-out public page modules on capture date.

Disclaimer: This is a public, logged-out harvest of candidate-controlled and official channels plus credible indexed public posts. It reports visible patterns and documented absences in the capture set; it does not infer intent, private strategy, or unobserved activity.
