### Prosecutorial Philosophy (CRITICAL FINDING)

Cunningham has a notably **strict law-and-order approach** compared to his predecessors:

#### Marijuana Prosecution Resumed (MAJOR POLICY SHIFT)
- Previous County Attorney Robert Anderson **paused/declined to file** possession of small amounts of marijuana cases
- **Anderson's reasoning:** The office receives more than 1,000 police reports every year but could only prosecute 600-700 cases. Anderson prioritized crimes with victims from the community: "In a misdemeanor marijuana case, the victim is the state of Kansas at large with no individual attached to the case." Anderson personally stated: *"My personal opinion is that marijuana and hemp should be legal, or at a very minimum decriminalized."*
- Anderson left the county attorney's office to **return to private practice** at [Anderson Law Office, LLC](https://www.andersonlawoffice.net/) in Hays
- Cunningham **reversed this policy**, stating: *"I tend to be of the philosophy that whether or not something is a crime is up to the Legislature. I have an obligation to enforce the law as it's written. I don't choose which ones are important and should be enforced and which ones aren't."*
- **However, with a practical caveat:** Due to limited resources, the office must prioritize. A person stopped with a marijuana pipe on the interstate "may not be prosecuted, especially if they are not citizens of Ellis County and imminent threat to lawful behavior."
- **Priority hierarchy:** (1) Death of a person, (2) crimes involving child victims, (3) sex crimes, (4) violent person crimes, (5) property crimes, (6) drug distribution crimes

#### Delta-8/Delta-10 THC Crackdown
- Stated he would **consider filing charges on hemp-derived products** (Delta-8, Delta-10), calling them "illegal hallucinogenics"
- Noted: "the county has not been active in holding people accountable on those on the county level" though "the city has been faithful in prosecuting those crimes"
- Under the previous county attorney, Delta-8 products were warned as illegal and pulled from area store shelves (Jan 2022)

#### Staffing Concerns

### Revenue Neutral Rate Mailings (NOTABLE ACCOMPLISHMENT)
- In March 2021, the Kansas Legislature passed K.S.A. 79-2988 requiring county clerks to send taxpayers notification of the revenue neutral rate (RNR) compared to proposed rates for each taxing subdivision
- Taxing subdivisions cannot exceed the RNR without first holding a public hearing and passing a resolution
- **Dreiling was the first clerk in Ellis County to prepare and send revenue-neutral rate mailings** to every taxpayer
- This was a new state requirement, and her implementation set the local precedent for tax transparency
- [Revenue Neutral Rate -- Hays Post](https://hayspost.com/posts/1003f2e5-b320-47b2-8fb1-4bbc626ef191)

1. Dreiling **closed the polling location at 601 Main** (closest to FHSU campus) citing low voter turnout
2. Combined that ward/precinct with another closed for **ADA compliance issues**
3. New combined polling location: **Messiah Lutheran Church**
4. **ACLU of Kansas launched a campaign** calling for an on-campus polling location at FHSU
5. FHSU student **Madison Albers** (from Goodland) presented a **petition with 480 signatures** to Dreiling
6. Petition presented by Albers plus Hays residents/FHSU graduates Anna Towns and Anniston Weber, accompanied by two ACLU of Kansas representatives
7. **WIBW (Topeka CBS affiliate)** covered the story: "Students, community members plead for polling location at Fort Hays State" (Nov 2, 2023)

#### Dreiling's Defense
- Stated "the numbers don't show for me to allow to spend taxpayer dollars" on a campus location
- Cited FHSU data: ~1,650 students live on campus, but **only 69 registered voters** in Ellis County live on campus
- Argued cost-benefit doesn't justify it

#### Challenger's Position
- **Democrat Laura Allen** ran against Dreiling in 2024 specifically on voting accessibility
- Allen favored campus polling location, argued students registered in Ellis County should be encouraged to vote locally, which could help with post-graduation retention
- Allen also criticized Dreiling for **removing ballot drop boxes from Victoria and Ellis** (small Ellis County communities)
- Dreiling cited cost of maintaining security at those drop boxes and their low usage

#### Additional Criticism
- A letter to the editor from a Democratic candidate was published criticizing Dreiling's election administration (details in [Hays Post](https://hayspost.com/posts/7c1c8817-1e3a-4c50-951e-895526935cf1))

## The R9 Ranch Water Project (Dougherty's Defining Legacy)
This is the single most important project of Dougherty's 19-year tenure. Overview:
- **The problem:** Hays sits over the depleting Ogallala Aquifer. The city's water supply is existentially threatened.
- **The solution:** In 1995, the city purchased the R9 Ranch -- 6,700 acres in Edwards County with 8,000 acre-feet of water rights, potentially providing 4,800 acre-feet of sustainable annual water supply.
- **The project:** Construct a 65-90 mile pipeline from the R9 wellfield to Hays and Russell. Estimated cost: **$144.9 million** (up from $75-80M estimate 5 years ago).
- **The fight:** Filed change-of-use application in 2015 (agricultural irrigation to municipal use). WaterPACK (agricultural irrigators group) has fought it through the legal system. Dougherty quote: "The opponents in this have succeeded in weaponizing the legal and judicial system against Hays and Russell."
- **Funding secured:** $15.8M Build Kansas grant (April 2025). Working on $50M FEMA BRIC grant application. FEMA providing help with federal grant application.
- **Legal battle:** Kansas Supreme Court heard oral arguments in December 2025. State legislature passed a water bill solidifying state control of water transfers.
- **Current status (March 2026):** Pipeline design wrapping up. Right-of-way permits being sought. Expected to bid early 2026. Dougherty quote: "Securing this Build Kansas grant marks a significant step forward."

## Water Conservation Program (National Recognition)
Under Dougherty's leadership, Hays became known as the **"California of Kansas"** for its aggressive water conservation:
- Gave away 20,000+ low-flow showerheads
- Hundreds of thousands of dollars for low-flow toilet programs
- "Cash-for-grass" terrascaping incentives
- Daytime restrictions on nonessential irrigation
- **Reduced city water use by one-third** beginning in the 1990s
- Built a water recycling plant
- Dougherty quote: "We have had to look to places like Las Vegas and Tucson and Phoenix" and "We stole our landscaping regulations from Utah."

## Strong Towns Connection

- Key insight: New growth -- typically seen as an asset -- could be a liability

- This is notable because Strong Towns advocates for fiscally responsible, walkable, incrementally developed cities -- NOT sprawl-driven growth.

## 8. STATEWIDE BALLOT MEASURES -- 2026

### Constitutional Amendment #1 -- ON THE PRIMARY BALLOT August 4, 2026
**Kansas Elections for Supreme Court Justices Amendment**
- Would provide for direct election of Kansas Supreme Court justices by voters
- Would abolish the existing Kansas Supreme Court Nominating Commission
- Justices' terms remain six years; vacancies filled by election
- Referred by the Kansas Legislature (requires 2/3 vote)

### Constitutional Amendment #2 -- ON THE GENERAL BALLOT November 3, 2026
**Citizenship Voting Requirement Amendment**
- Would amend the Kansas Constitution to state that only U.S. citizens may vote
- Text: "No person shall be deemed a qualified elector unless such person: (1) Is a citizen of the United States..."

**Confidence: HIGH** -- Both measures have been certified to appear on the ballot.
