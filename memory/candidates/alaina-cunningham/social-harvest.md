## Current Role / Status

The City of Hays official commission page, crawled the week of capture, lists Mason Ruder as mayor and Alaina Cunningham as vice mayor. The same page describes the commission as a policy-making body in a commission-manager government and says commissioners are elected at large every two years. ([City of Hays](https://www.haysusa.com/269/City-Commission))

Hays Post reported that on January 8, 2026, the commission reorganized after new commissioners were sworn in; the full commission voted to elect Mason Ruder mayor and Cunningham vice mayor. ([Hays Post, Jan. 10, 2026](https://hayspost.com/posts/21208822-3905-4a26-a33d-5003cf6f8f08)) Tiger Media Network separately reported that Cunningham and Ruder were not up for election in the 2025 city election and that Cunningham was nominated and elected vice mayor. ([Tiger Media Network, Jan. 12, 2026](https://tigermedianet.com/?p=93385))

## Public Platform Signals

Cunningham's public, attributable message is not organized around a campaign social feed. It is mostly dispersed across local media questionnaires, city meeting coverage, city records, and podcast listings.

The earliest reviewed public service message is her appointment and swearing-in. Hays Post reported that she was unanimously appointed to fill Michael Berges' vacated Hays City Commission seat and quoted her after being sworn in: "I just want to say thank you for letting me join you, and I'm very excited to serve our community." ([Hays Post, Jan. 10, 2023](https://hayspost.com/posts/c8cd14ed-94b7-4507-8a9c-ad71038ac5b6))

Her 2023 candidate Q&A is the densest first-person source. She framed her run around public service, saying she wanted to make positive change and that she tries to apply the motto "Do Good" in all areas of life. Her stated priorities were sustainable water, economic growth, housing supply, youth retention, retirees and aging residents, budget discipline, infrastructure, and quality of life. ([Hays Post, Oct. 25, 2023](https://hayspost.com/posts/5552ce0e-bfbc-418e-addf-a4318ee25055))

Tiger Media Network's 2023 candidate feature repeats the service frame and adds biography: she moved to Hays in August 2010, raised three children in the city, works as a registered nurse and group fitness instructor, and has served with Downtown Hays Development Corporation, HaysMed Foundation, Hays High Academic Booster Club, and O'Loughlin PTA. ([Tiger Media Network, 2023](https://tigermedianet.com/?p=75270))

After the November 2023 election, Hays Post reported that Cunningham received 27 percent of the vote and won a four-year term. She said she was "Truly honored to be chosen by the voters" and wanted to keep "bettering our community" and "doing good for Hays as a whole." ([Hays Post, Nov. 8, 2023](https://hayspost.com/posts/5c04aec5-5ab3-413c-8844-d90e91aa0d31))

On development incentives, she repeatedly frames incentives as a practical tool for growth rather than an ideological talking point. In late 2023, after commissioners approved development-related agreements, she said, "We've all agreed that growth is what we want," and argued that the first developer paying infrastructure costs should have a chance to recoup costs. ([Hays Post](https://hayspost.com/posts/eeb37c70-d05e-427e-bdb6-b673010bd8af)) In November 2025, she said of Agiliti incentives that the economic benefit to the community was valuable. ([Hays Post, Nov. 15, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/4d49c8ec-b445-4b28-a49c-d154c709104f)) In another 2025 incentives story, she said Hays is desirable but that conversations with developers show that "without incentives, we just can't get anyone here." ([Hays Post](https://hayspost.com/posts/a308442f-a0fc-49ea-b6a1-b905513a368b))

On water, Cunningham's own statements put R9 and long-term water supply in survival terms. In her 2023 Q&A, she said securing a sustainable water source was one of the top issues facing Hays and praised conservation and the R9 framework. ([Hays Post, Oct. 25, 2023](https://hayspost.com/posts/5552ce0e-bfbc-418e-addf-a4318ee25055)) At a 2025 Hays-Russell R9 update, she said, "This is about sustainability and survival," and "without it, we don't survive." ([Hays Post, Aug. 29, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/72cef8d3-79bf-4085-b070-bccbdc589f9d))

On budget and tax tradeoffs, she is visible as a constrained-budget voice. During 2026 budget approval, she told staff, "You brought us a constrained budget which is what we asked for." ([Hays Post, Sep. 12, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/7d615ae1-9b48-4ebd-9200-7c593c85db69)) When Rep. Barb Wasinger asked local officials for property-tax-relief ideas, Cunningham warned, "When we eliminate sources of funding, we're just shifting the burden." ([Hays Post, Dec. 20, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/31a9d9f8-0883-480f-aada-22ac084f09b9)) In April 2026 budget-prep coverage, she said early public awareness matters because property-tax caps can affect city services and amenities. ([Hays Post, Apr. 6, 2026](https://hayspost.com/posts/87cea43b-4017-4065-a707-e19a1389beeb))

On resident consultation and local ordinance issues, her public remarks are procedural. During a 2025 fireworks survey discussion, she said fireworks are a "hot topic" and asked residents to give opinions so commissioners could better understand what the community wants. ([Hays Post, May 10, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/26608269-25e1-4f29-b80f-d2edbb3bba3d)) In a backyard-chickens story, she relayed that Cottonwood Extension could provide educational materials if the ordinance changed. ([Hays Post](https://hayspost.com/posts/c8f8c9de-3d46-470d-8ae1-7efbe793a460))

## Methodology

Date range covered: December 27, 2022 through May 11, 2026. The start date is the public appointment period for Cunningham's Hays City Commission service; the endpoint is the capture date. Older biography and civic-service records were used only where tied to current role context or current public pages.

Platforms and source classes surveyed: City of Hays commission page, agenda/minutes packets, city audio/video entry points, Hays Post, Tiger Media Network, HaysMed, Downtown Hays Development Corporation, Facebook, LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Bluesky, Threads, Truth Social, Gab, Gettr, Substack, Medium, Reddit, podcast indexes, and broad public web search.

Items examined: 21 accessible public items, including 18 role-relevant or message-bearing items and 3 biography/status/presence items. Login-walled, script-blocked, or otherwise inaccessible social feeds were not counted as reviewed content. Engagement is reported only where visible and attributable in public capture.

Disclaimer: This is a public, logged-out harvest of candidate-controlled or candidate-attributed messaging plus official records and credible local coverage. It reports visible patterns and documented absences in the capture set; it does not infer private views, unobserved social activity, or inaccessible account content.
