## Notable Quotes And Remarks

- On why he ran, Leiker cited zoning board, Cottonwood Extension, oil-industry, rural-fire, and family-farm experience, saying he was proud to be from Ellis County and looked forward to serving it. Source: [Hays Post, Apr. 12, 2022](https://hayspost.com/posts/aedbf614-0ea1-4a7c-8932-156b271a4709).
- On sales tax, he said the county should leave the sales tax alone and make it permanent before sunset, while using the funds for capital improvements and residents' quality of life. Source: [Hays Post candidate Q&A, Jul. 19, 2022](https://hayspost.com/posts/599cb8a3-fed5-4c96-bb58-7079dcedac17).
- On roads and bridges, he said infrastructure funds were too far behind to use for other purposes and later said he wanted more focus on roads, bridges, and growth infrastructure. Sources: [Hays Post candidate Q&A](https://hayspost.com/posts/599cb8a3-fed5-4c96-bb58-7079dcedac17) and [Hays Post canvass coverage](https://hayspost.com/posts/6e260438-27db-4402-bf8e-e8df621996d4).
- On economic development, he said the county should be proactive and that stable taxes, available capital, and infrastructure investment would help business follow. Source: [Hays Post candidate Q&A](https://hayspost.com/posts/599cb8a3-fed5-4c96-bb58-7079dcedac17).
- On citizen governance, he described himself as someone who built his life around Ellis County and had done the "dirty, grimy, hard jobs" many taxpayers do. Source: [Hays Post candidate Q&A](https://hayspost.com/posts/599cb8a3-fed5-4c96-bb58-7079dcedac17).
- On property rights, he criticized a 2021 zoning denial after zoning-board approval and said government should have very little authority over what people do on their property apart from illegal actions. Source: [Hays Post candidate Q&A](https://hayspost.com/posts/599cb8a3-fed5-4c96-bb58-7079dcedac17).
- On commission expansion, he said more government is not a good thing and more managers at the top are not the answer. Source: [Hays Post candidate Q&A](https://hayspost.com/posts/599cb8a3-fed5-4c96-bb58-7079dcedac17).
- On agriculture, he told Ingram's that producer-consumer partnerships were an opportunity after COVID exposed food-supply fragility, and that agriculture's values of hard work and perseverance do not change. Source: [Ingram's, March 2021](https://ingrams.com/article/50-kansans-you-should-know-2021/).
- On cattle technology, he said MyAnIML could spread cattle-health expertise across the farm and that low-stress, less-invasive management drew him to the tool. Source: [MyAnIML case study, Jul. 19, 2023](https://myaniml.com/Kansas_Rancher_Installs_MyAnIML_to_Save.pdf).
- On rural road maintenance, he said Ellis County promoted growth in subdivisions but did not promote growth in public works to handle those added residents. Source: [Hays Post, Feb. 29, 2024](https://hayspost.com/posts/0ec5af1e-3acd-4f02-9842-bf61a57eae9a).
- On the 2025 budget, he said the commission tries to limit tax-dollar impact, but also warned against deferring bridge and road needs until failures or accidents happen. Source: [Hays Post, Oct. 1, 2024](https://hayspost.com/posts/d81583db-0414-44c6-8dac-7d4ad565ed13).
- On city-county road collaboration north of I-70, he called the Vineyard Road project forward-looking and said the future of Hays was developing in that direction. Source: [Hays Post, Aug. 16, 2024](https://hayspost.com/posts/2584b551-eb90-4e5b-abbf-fd3ef6cab0bc).
- On the 2026 budget draft, he said a mill increase gave the county flexibility for a soft landing if state or federal grants were pulled. Source: [Hays Post, Aug. 14, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/7c57b3c8-1314-4bd7-8501-f559ff7c176d).
- On property tax relief, he said counties keep losing tools and then get told they are not doing it right. Source: [Hays Post, Dec. 22, 2025](https://hayspost.com/posts/0dc3bf76-bd9f-4190-a0c7-231ca8172d18).
- On solar project review, he said commissioners were not experts and wanted expert help to guide the best decision for constituents. Source: [Hays Post, Jan. 13, 2026](https://hayspost.com/posts/18d99844-46b7-4bc0-a558-9d9a87fa92e6).

## Methodology

I searched for Nathan Leiker, Nathan D. Leiker, Ellis County Commissioner District 3, L5 Farms, Hays Post coverage, Ellis County commission pages, agenda/minutes/media, public social accounts, LinkedIn, Facebook, X/Twitter, Instagram, Threads, TikTok, YouTube, agriculture profiles, Kansas ranch/farm technology references, local election results, and obvious platform absences. I prioritized official county pages, candidate-authored Q&A answers, attributed direct quotes, public meeting coverage, and profile-controlled or attributed agriculture/professional remarks. I did not fabricate inaccessible social content; where platforms were login-walled, script-blocked, ambiguous, or not publicly indexed, this harvest says so and relies only on accessible previews, official links, and attributed reporting.
