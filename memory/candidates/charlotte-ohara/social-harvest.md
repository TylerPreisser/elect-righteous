## Public Platform Signals

The campaign website is the dominant candidate-controlled platform. It combines a governor campaign landing page, a donation page, and an unusually large first-person opinion archive. The homepage says she is running to make Kansas a place "to come home to," bring DOGE-style spending restraint to Kansas, stop tax incentives that she says increase property taxes, demand transparency, elect judges in direct partisan elections, remove DEI/WOKE/CRT from public schools, and protect land and quality of life from industrial energy projects and eminent domain. ([source](https://oharaforkansas.com/))

The site biography presents O'Hara as a former general contractor/developer, small-business owner, former Kansas House member, former Johnson County commissioner, mother, grandmother, and person whose faith is central to public life. It says her family business survived the COVID shutdown "only by the Grace of God," and says strong families and faith communities are building blocks for Kansas. ([source](https://oharaforkansas.com/))

The campaign archive contained 71 public posts in the WordPress API on capture. The earliest listed post was "A Sobering Look at Abortion Statistics in Kansas" on 2025-01-14; the latest listed post was "The Great Property Tax Shift" on 2026-05-05. ([archive source](https://oharaforkansas.com/wp-json/wp/v2/posts?per_page=100&_fields=date,slug,link,title.rendered,categories))

The highest-frequency campaign-archive themes are Kansas issues, transparency, elections/voting, taxes, Johnson County spending, public safety, and property taxes. WordPress category counts are overlapping because most posts carry multiple categories: Kansas Issues 70, Transparency 57, Elections 50, Voting 45, Taxes 31, JoCo Spending 23, Public Safety 20, Property Taxes 19, Safety 15, JoCo Budget 12, BOCC 4, Podcasts 1, Uncategorized 1. ([category source](https://oharaforkansas.com/wp-json/wp/v2/categories?per_page=100))

The campaign's May 5, 2026 post "The Great Property Tax Shift" is a representative recent piece. O'Hara argues residential homeowners are carrying a larger share of Johnson County property-tax revenue, attacks STAR bonds, TIFs, IRBs, HPIP and PEAK programs, and proposes prohibiting local governments from hiring lobbyists, sunsetting incentive programs, stopping tax exemptions/credits, and eliminating property taxes through major spending cuts and audits. ([source](https://oharaforkansas.com/the-great-property-tax-shift/))

The March 24, 2026 post "Election Integrity: the Ghost of 2020" tied election-integrity messaging to opposition to the sports authority/STAR bond package and asked readers to contact state senators. ([source](https://oharaforkansas.com/election-integrity-the-ghost-of-2020/))

The April 28, 2026 post "Dismantling the Kansas Department of Education" argues education should be local and criticizes what she describes as tax-incentive funded candidates and the state education bureaucracy. ([source](https://oharaforkansas.com/dismantling-the-kansas-department-of-education/))

The Feb. 17, 2026 post "When Did You Begin" is a pro-life poem that asks when human dignity begins, reinforcing abortion as a recurring campaign theme. ([source](https://oharaforkansas.com/when-did-you-begin/))

The May 2, 2025 post "Men in Women's YMCA Locker Rooms?" frames sex-segregated spaces through O'Hara's religious and social-conservative worldview. ([source](https://oharaforkansas.com/men-in-womens-ymca-locker-rooms/))

The July 22, 2025 post "The Wonder Drug War - Ivermectin, COVID and Politics!" says she is not a doctor but supports the "health freedom movement" in the politics around ivermectin. ([source](https://oharaforkansas.com/the-wonder-drug-war-ivermectin-covid-and-politics/))

The Jan. 28, 2025 post "A New Era for Kansas and American Politics" praises President Trump's executive-order pace and presents it as a model for breaking both Democrats and the "old Republican guard." ([source](https://oharaforkansas.com/trump-vs-the-old-guard-a-new-era-for-kansas-and-american-politics/))

The public Facebook page metadata is much thinner than the campaign site but confirms a current page identity: "O'Hara for Kansas," 1,513 likes, 389 talking about this, and the description "O'Hara for Kansas Governor." Individual Facebook posts were not reliably inventoryable in logged-out capture. ([source](https://www.facebook.com/oharaforjoco/))

## Highest Visible Engagement

The highest visible public engagement metric in the capture was Facebook page-level metadata: 1,513 likes and 389 talking about this for "O'Hara for Kansas" on 2026-05-11. The campaign website exposed 71 posts but no reliable public engagement counts. No verified post-level engagement was visible for Facebook, and no candidate-controlled X, Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Bluesky, Truth Social, Gab, Gettr, Substack, or Reddit account was found.

## Repost / Amplification Review

The campaign website is primarily original commentary rather than a repost feed. It does amplify outside references inside posts, including outside videos/podcasts, WatchDog Lab/Substack material on voter rolls, and news/policy references. Logged-out Facebook capture did not expose a reliable repost/share inventory, so no Facebook repost total is reported.

## Visible Absences

Across the accessible public set reviewed, there was no clear candidate-controlled presence on X, Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Bluesky, Truth Social, Gab, Gettr, Substack, or Reddit. The campaign website did not visibly expose current social links in the captured homepage text, even though the Facebook page at the old JoCo URL is active under "O'Hara for Kansas."

Issue absences are more limited because O'Hara's archive is large. In the accessible set reviewed, no dominant candidate-controlled messaging centered Medicaid expansion, hospital finance, child care, foster care, housing affordability, broadband, higher-education affordability, water policy, the Ogallala aquifer, or criminal-justice sentencing detail. Those absences are limited to the capture set and should not be read as proof that she has no position.

## Methodology

Date range covered: 2025-01-14 to 2026-05-11 for candidate-controlled posts, public social metadata, and indexed public coverage. Current capture was performed on 2026-05-11. The latest campaign-site post found in the public WordPress API was 2026-05-05.

Platforms surveyed: campaign website, campaign WordPress archive/API, Facebook, JoCo election profile, X/Twitter, Instagram, YouTube, Bluesky, TikTok, Truth Social, Gab, Gettr, Substack, Reddit, public search, Kansas Reflector, Johnson County Post, and existing candidate-data source links.

Items examined: 18 accessible public/campaign/profile/news items, including 71 campaign-site post records and candidate-controlled category metadata. Login-walled, script-blocked, or otherwise inaccessible social feeds were marked as limited and not counted as post-level reviewed. Engagement was reported only where visible in public metadata on capture date.

Disclaimer: This is a public, logged-out harvest of candidate-controlled and official/public channels plus credible indexed public posts. It reports visible patterns and documented absences in the capture set; it does not infer intent, private strategy, or unobserved activity.
