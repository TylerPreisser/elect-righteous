## Public Platform Signals

Colyer's active campaign footprint is split between an older campaign domain, [jeffcolyer.com](https://jeffcolyer.com/), and a 2026-specific domain, [colyerforgovernor.com](https://www.colyerforgovernor.com/). The older site links his public X, Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube accounts and foregrounds action items on critical race theory, private property rights, abortion, KU stem-cell funding, and oil/gas policy. The homepage identifies him as a fifth-generation Kansan from Hays and says he is "dedicated to making a difference in people's lives" ([source](https://jeffcolyer.com/)).

The 2026 campaign site is more polished and explicitly primary-oriented. It leads with Trump validation, "Faith & Family First," Colyer's status as Kansas's 47th governor, his appointment by Trump to a rural-health advisory role, and his service as Trump's 2024 Kansas chair. It lists five campaign pillars: lower costs/higher pay, safer communities, freedom to thrive, healthy rural Kansas, and honest accountable government ([source](https://www.colyerforgovernor.com/)).

The public X mirror for [@DrJeffColyer](https://mobile.twstalker.com/DrJeffColyer) listed the profile as of capture with roughly 5K followers, 2K tweets, and a bio describing him as the 47th governor of Kansas and Trump's 2024 Kansas chair. Recent indexed posts were heavily political, but the immediately visible feed also included sports, military, condolence, and patriotic posts, which means the current public X signal is a mix of campaign messaging and general conservative/community identity.

Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, and LinkedIn were present but did not expose reliable logged-out post inventories. They are counted as platform presence, not as reviewed post archives. No candidate-controlled TikTok, Bluesky, Truth Social, Gab, Gettr, Substack, or Reddit account was found in public search.

## Methodology

Date range covered: 2025-05-12 to 2026-05-11 for current campaign and social presence, with selected still-live 2021 campaign archive pages noted separately because they remain linked from the current campaign site. Platforms surveyed: campaign websites, X, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, LinkedIn, TikTok, Bluesky, Truth Social, Gab, Gettr, Substack, Reddit, public search, and credible indexed news coverage. Items examined: 29 accessible original/profile/public-record items and 1 clearly visible retweet/quote-amplification cluster. Direct X, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, and LinkedIn feeds were limited by logged-out/script/login walls; indexed mirrors and public search were used only where they exposed verifiable public text and metrics. Engagement was used only where visible in public metadata on capture date.

Disclaimer: This harvest reports public, candidate-controlled or candidate-attributed signals visible to a logged-out researcher on 2026-05-11. It is not a complete archive of every post, deletion, paid ad, email, private group post, story, livestream, or login-only platform interaction.
