## 3. COMPLETE VOTING RECORD -- KEY VOTES

### January 6 / 2020 Election Certification
- **VOTED AGAINST certifying electoral votes** from Arizona AND Pennsylvania on January 6, 2021

  - Pennsylvania objection rejected 138-282

- **Justification:** "Our country must have free and fair elections and credible allegations of fraud must be investigated."

## 7. LIEUTENANT GOVERNOR (2018-2019)

### Timeline:
1. **January 31, 2018:** Governor Sam Brownback resigns to become U.S. Ambassador at Large for International Religious Freedom
2. **January 31, 2018:** Lt. Governor Jeff Colyer ascends to Governor
3. **February 13, 2018:** Governor Colyer appoints Tracey Mann as Lt. Governor
4. **February 14, 2018:** Mann sworn in as 50th Lt. Governor of Kansas
5. **August 7, 2018:** Colyer loses Republican primary to Kris Kobach by approximately 345 votes (closest statewide race in Kansas history)
6. **November 6, 2018:** Democrat Laura Kelly defeats Kobach for Governor
7. **January 14, 2019:** Mann leaves office with Colyer

### Why It Was Brief:
Mann served less than 11 months. He was appointed to fill a vacancy created by a chain of events (Brownback resignation -> Colyer promotion -> Mann appointment). Colyer then lost the primary to Kris Kobach in one of the closest races in Kansas history. Kobach then lost the general election to Laura Kelly. Mann's tenure was entirely derivative of Colyer's brief governorship.

### What He Did as Lt. Gov:
- Visited businesses across Kansas (documented visits to places like Ft. Scott Munitions)
- No major policy initiatives in the brief tenure

Sources: [KCUR](https://www.kcur.org/politics-elections-and-government/2018-02-13/colyer-picks-real-estate-operator-tracey-mann-as-kansas-lt-governor) | [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tracey_Mann) | [Sentinel KS](https://sentinelksmo.org/tracey-mann-sworn-lieutenant-governor/)

## OVERALL ASSESSMENT

### Strengths:
1. **Agricultural expertise is genuine.** Fifth-generation farmer, ag economics degree, understands the issues, chairs a relevant subcommittee.
2. **Constituent engagement above average** for a rural district -- 60-county annual tours, 186+ town halls.
3. **Safe seat.** KS-01 is one of the most Republican districts in the country. He wins by 30+ points.
4. **Aligned with Trump base.** In a district that went heavily for Trump, his full-throated support is an asset.

### Vulnerabilities:
1. **Birtherism history** can be resurfaced. The Hutchinson News withdrawal is documented.
2. **January 6 objection vote** is a permanent record that could matter in a general election or with moderate/independent voters.
3. **ROTOR Act vote** is a fresh wound -- voting against aviation safety after a crash involving a Kansas flight is optically terrible.
4. **Campaign finance dependency on PACs/large donors** (56.71% PAC, only 0.72% small dollar) -- could be framed as "bought and paid for" in a populist environment.
5. **No signature legislation enacted.** After 5+ years in Congress, no Mann-authored bill has become law. His impact is through committee work and amendments, which is harder to campaign on.
6. **Net worth of $6M** in a district with significant rural poverty could be an optics issue if framed correctly.
7. **"Party-line voter" label** -- 96% Heritage Action, 19th most conservative in the House. Extremely limited bipartisan work.

### Bottom Line:
Tracey Mann is a solidly conservative, Trump-aligned Republican congressman in an extremely safe district. He is effective on agriculture committee work but has no landmark legislation to his name. His vulnerabilities (birtherism, Jan 6 vote, ROTOR Act, PAC dependency) exist but are unlikely to matter in KS-01's heavily Republican electorate. A serious challenge would need to come from a Republican primary, not the general election. The 2026 Democratic challengers from Lawrence face near-impossible math in this district.
